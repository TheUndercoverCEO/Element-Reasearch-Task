Introduction

In this website, you will learn about the Elements Americium(Am, 95) and Radium(Ra, 88). These two elements are made artificially and are not naturally occurring. Let's begin.

Americium

Brief overview

Americium(Am) was discovered in 1944 by the American scientists Glenn T. Seaborg, Ralph A. James, Leon O. Morgan and Albert Ghiorso. This work was carried out at the University of Chicago's Metallurgical Laboratory, now known as Argonne National Laboratory. They produced Americium by bombarding plutonium-239, an isotope of plutonium, with high energy neutrons. This formed plutonium-240, which was itself bombarded with neutrons. The plutonium-240 changed into plutonium-241, which then decayed into americium-241 through beta decay. *See Glossary

Argonnes Science and Technology
Scientific overview

Americium's Atomic number is 95 and weight number is 243. The Element Americium is found on the seventh period on the periodic table and belongs to the Actinoids group. Americium, at room temperature, is a solid with a melting point of 1176°C and a boiling point of 2011°C. Americium is, in fact, a metal and Lustrous. Americium is a Radioactive metal and is Dangerous. Americium can be used in compounds, for example:

  • Americium oxide: AmO
  • Americium dioxide: AmO2
  • Diamericium trioxide: Am2O3

Radium

Brief overview

Radium(Ra) was discovered in 1898 by Marie Sklodowska Curie, a Polish chemist, and Pierre Curie, a French chemist in Bohemia (Today, the Czech Republic). They produced Radium by refining pitchblende, a matiral that contains Uranium. *See Glossary

Scientific overview

Radium's Atomic number is 88 and weight number is 226. The Element Radium is found on the seventh period on the periodic table and belongs to the Alkaline Earth Metal group. Radium, at room temperature, is a solid with a melting point of 700°C and a boiling point of 1140°C. Radium is, in fact, a metal and Lustrous. Radium is a Radioactive metal and is Dangerous. Radium can be used in compounds, for example:

  • Radium oxide: RaO
  • radium chloride: RaCl2
  • radium bromide: RaBr2
